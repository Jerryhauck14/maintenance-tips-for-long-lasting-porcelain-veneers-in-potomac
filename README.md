## 1. Follow a Consistent Oral Hygiene Routine

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1100/format:webp/1*lIoYztKXEAQtrvBi5gh15Q.png)

Good oral hygiene is the foundation of caring for Porcelain Veneers Potomac patients. Veneers themselves do not decay, but the natural teeth underneath do.

Brush twice a day and floss once daily to prevent plaque buildup and maintain gum health. Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and non-abrasive toothpaste to avoid scratching the veneer surface.

## 2. Choose the Right Dental Products

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1100/format:webp/1*DlMBWEuKeRnBhZO_z2UyCg.png)

Not all dental products are safe for porcelain restorations. 

For the best results:

* Avoid whitening toothpaste (can increase surface wear).
* Use alcohol-free mouthwash to prevent dryness or adhesive weakening.
* Ask your dentistry provider at Standard Dental LLC for veneer-safe product recommendations.

## 3. Avoid Hard Foods and Habits That Cause Damage

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1100/format:webp/1*QdIEntdrYVGUHWTMHLS_Xg.png)

Porcelain veneers are strong, but they are not indestructible. Protect them by avoiding:

* Chewing on ice or hard candies
* Biting nails or using teeth as tools
* Opening packages with your teeth
* These habits can cause veneer chips or fractures.

## 4. Protect Your Veneers During Sports and Night Grinding

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1100/format:webp/1*nnhnQqor3P2bwCGtIckGLg.png)

If you grind your teeth at night (bruxism) or play sports, your Porcelain Veneers Potomac treatment needs extra protection.

Wearing a custom night guard or athletic mouthguard prevents unnecessary pressure that can damage veneers. Standard Dental LLC can create custom-fit guards that ensure comfort and total protection.

## 5. Be Mindful With Stain-Causing Foods and Drinks

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1100/format:webp/1*ND51OWMlo7WZ6aDsPvom1A.png)

Porcelain doesn’t stain easily, but the surrounding natural teeth can. This can make your veneers stand out unnaturally over time.

Limit:

* Coffee & tea
* Red wine
* Dark berries
* Tobacco products

If you consume them, rinse your mouth with water afterward.
